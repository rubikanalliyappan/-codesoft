Traditional candies of Tamilnadu

Tamil Nadu, a state in southern India, has a rich tradition of making candies using natural ingredients and traditional methods. Some of the popular traditional candies from Tamil Nadu include:

Kamarkattu:

A dark brown candy made of jaggery and coconut, sometimes with added dry ginger and cardamom. It is known for being tough on the teeth and aiding digestion.

Elandhavadai:

A tangy, sweet, salty, and sour candy made of jaggery and Indian dates or jujube. It is a great example of the Tamil arusuvai concept, which includes all six tastes in a single piece.

Thaen mittai:

Made of sugarcane syrup, it provides a healthier alternative to preservative-laden, plastic-wrapped toffees.

Kadalai mittai:

A classic candy from Kovilpatti, made of peanuts and jaggery. It is a perennially loved candy and provides protein and iron.

Inji morappa:

A yellow, ginger-based candy made of dry ginger, sugar, and cumin seeds. It helps with indigestion, acidity, sore throat, and rudimentary food poisoning.

These traditional candies are made without chemical preservatives and contribute to better health. However, due to the popularity of branded chocolates, they have become less common in the market. Some of these candies, like kamarkattu and elandhavadai, are slowly making a comeback, and enthusiasts hope that they will become more widely available in the future.
